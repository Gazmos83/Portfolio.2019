Frequently Asked Questions

My day starts off with a morning standup, even if I'm working alone. It helps me prioritize my goals.

In my experience the hardests tasks are best dealt with with a fresh mind so I typically try to tackle the most challenging coding obstacles first. Then I trickle down to the more familiar tasks. It's quite often the case, that the solution will pop into my head early on the next day after a good nights sleep. Therefore, any serious unsolved problems are reviewed one last time within the last 30 minutes of my work day to keep the problem in the back of my head.

My choice to become a Web Developer is one part for the challenge and one part for the necessity. Solving a challenging puzzle is a rewarding feeling and having an online presence nowadays is a necessity for almost any line of work. In Web Development, I get the best of both worlds.
